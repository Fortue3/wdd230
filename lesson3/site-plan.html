Overview

Purpose

To promote business ventures in the city of Gweru and also raise or capital for newly formed businesses or company start ups

Personas

Chambers of commerce typically target a few key personas, including:

  • Small business owners: These are the core audience for a chamber of commerce. They offer services that help small business owners grow their businesses, such as networking opportunities, marketing and advertising advice, and access to training and education.
  • Local leaders: Chambers of commerce often work closely with local government officials, community leaders, and other stakeholders to advocate for the needs and interests of the business community.
  • Entrepreneurs: Many chambers of commerce offer resources and support specifically for entrepreneurs, such as mentoring, business plan development assistance and access to funding opportunities.
  • Community members: Chambers of commerce often provide information and resources about the community, and they may serve as a liaison between businesses and community members.
  • Investors: Some chambers of commerce may also target investors, such as venture capitalists, private equity firms, and angel investors, as they may be interested in learning more about business opportunities in a particular area.

Target Audience

The target audience of a chamber of commerce is typically local businesses and business owners.
This can include small businesses, entrepreneurs, startups, and established companies of all sizes and industries.
They also target local leaders, community members, investors, entrepreneurs and economic developers.
They aim to provide resources and support for these businesses, such as networking opportunities, training, and advocacy on behalf of the business community.
Additionally, chambers of commerce may also target members of the local community, as they often provide information and resources about the community and serve as a liaison between businesses and community members.

Scenarios

Here are a few examples of scenarios in which a chamber of commerce might be involved:
  • Networking events: Chambers of commerce often host networking events, such as mixers or networking breakfasts, to bring local businesses together and provide opportunities for them to connect and build relationships.
  • Business advocacy: Chambers of commerce may advocate on behalf of local businesses, for example, by lobbying local government officials for pro-business policies or by representing the interests of businesses in discussions with community leaders.
  • Business education: Chambers of commerce may offer training and education opportunities for local business owners, such as workshops on marketing, accounting, or human resources.
  • Community events: Chambers of commerce may organize or participate in community events, such as festivals, parades, or charity fundraisers, to promote the local business community and build a sense of community.
  • Economic development: Chambers of commerce may help attract new businesses to a community and assist existing businesses to expand, which can help create jobs and stimulate economic growth.
  • Referral service: Chambers of commerce also help business owners to connect with other businesses, by providing member's information through their website or directory
